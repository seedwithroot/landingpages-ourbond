# Bond - Meta Ads Brand Guidelines

**Last Updated:** April 13, 2026

---

## Brand Voice

**Core Positioning:**  
Elite-level personal security made accessible. Professional, trustworthy, but not intimidating.

**Tone Attributes:**
- **Confident** — Not fearful. We provide assurance, not alarm.
- **Accessible** — Professional security, consumer-friendly interface.
- **Direct** — Clear language, no jargon. Say what you mean.
- **Empowering** — Users feel safer and more in control, not dependent.

**Avoid:**
- Fear-mongering or alarmist language
- Overly technical security jargon
- Condescending "you need protection" messaging
- Generic safety platitudes

---

## Visual Style

**Photography Direction:**
- **Real, not stock** — Authentic moments, not posed safety scenes
- **Confident subjects** — People walking with purpose, not looking over shoulder
- **Urban, relatable settings** — City streets, campuses, familiar environments
- **Warm, cinematic lighting** — Not harsh or clinical
- **High contrast** — Dark backgrounds (often pure black) to make text pop

**Color Palette:**
- **Primary:** Deep blacks, clean whites
- **Accent:** Warm amber/gold (streetlight glow, app interface)
- **Supporting:** Cool blues (trust, technology), subtle teals

**Typography:**
- **Headlines:** Bold, modern sans-serif (high contrast, readable at small sizes)
- **Body:** Clean, legible sans-serif
- **Testimonials:** Quotation marks as design element, clear attribution

**Bond Logo Usage:**
- Small, unobtrusive placement (corner)
- Don't compete with primary message
- Consistent position across ad formats

## Messaging Frameworks

### Problem-Aware Messaging
**When to use:** Top of funnel, cold audiences  
**Structure:** Problem → Solution → Outcome  
**Example:** "Walking home alone? Bond monitors your route. A live agent responds if needed."

### Outcome-Led Messaging
**When to use:** Awareness, aspiration  
**Structure:** Desired state → How Bond delivers it  
**Example:** "Never walk alone again. Live agents watch your route until you're safely home."

### Proof-Led Messaging
**When to use:** Mid-funnel, remarketing, credibility building  
**Structure:** Testimonial/Authority → What it means for you  
**Example:** "Trusted by the former NYPD Commissioner. 24/7 personal security agents in your pocket."

### Feature-Led Messaging
**When to use:** Education, carousel, conversion  
**Structure:** Feature → Benefit → Why it matters  
**Example:** "Track Me On The Go monitors your route and confirms safe arrival. If you deviate or don't arrive, we check in."

These are what separate Bond from competitors. Feature them prominently.

1. **Live agents, not just automation** — Real security professionals, 24/7
2. **Preventative, not just reactive** — Act before situations reach 911 level
3. **Elite expertise, consumer access** — Former FBI, Secret Service, NYPD pros
4. **Multiple protection layers** — Video, GPS, voice, on-demand bodyguards
5. **Surprisingly affordable** — Elite security for less than a gym membership

## Image Rights & Requirements

**Testimonial Ads:**
- If using real customer names (Ken P., Jennifer J.), need photo release
- If using stock photography for archetype, keep generic enough for compliance
- Expert testimonials (Raymond Kelly, Bill Craig) — verify usage rights

**Product Screenshots:**
- Always use current app interface
- Keep UI clean and readable even at small sizes
- Show real features, not placeholder content

**Lifestyle Photography:**
- Diverse representation across age, gender, ethnicity
- Urban settings should be relatable (not luxury-only or dangerous-looking)
- Evening/night scenes should feel safe, not threatening

## Compliance & Legal

**Required Disclaimers:**
- Pricing subject to change
- Free trial requires credit card (if applicable)
- Emergency services coordination "when appropriate" (not guaranteed)

**Avoid Claims:**
- "Guaranteed safety" or "100% safe"
- "Prevent all crime" or absolute promises
- Specific response times (unless backed by SLA)

**Approved Claims:**
- "24/7 agents available"
- "Former [agency] professionals" (if verified)
- Customer testimonials (with permission)
- "Monitoring" and "coordinating with authorities" (not "replacing 911")

## CTA Best Practices

**Primary CTAs (in order of conversion strength):**
1. "Try Free for 3 Days"
2. "Download Bond"
3. "Get the App"
4. "Start Your Free Trial"

**Avoid:**
- "Learn More" (too vague)
- "Sign Up" (too transactional)
- "Get Started" (overused, no specificity)

**When to use which:**
- **Try Free for 3 Days** — Best for conversion, creates urgency, low commitment
- **Download Bond** — Simple, direct, works for any stage
- **Get the App** — Similar to download, slightly softer
- **Start Your Free Trial** — Good for premium plans, emphasizes no risk
