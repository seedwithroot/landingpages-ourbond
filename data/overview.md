## Competitors and Alternatives

Bond/OurBond sits at the intersection of personal safety apps, corporate duty‑of‑care platforms, and security/concierge services. There is no single one‑to‑one competitor, but they overlap with several categories:

### Personal safety apps (consumer‑focused)

- Noonlight: App that can silently call police and share location when a user triggers or fails to cancel an alarm; integrates with other apps and devices. [ourbond](https://www.ourbond.com/how-it-works/)
- Safe connectivity features in Life360 and similar family safety apps also overlap at the “monitoring and alerts” level, though with less emphasis on live agents.

### Corporate duty‑of‑care and travel risk platforms

- Everbridge and similar critical event management tools provide mass notification and incident coordination for enterprises and governments rather than individual live‑agent escorts, but they compete for safety budgets.  
- Travel risk providers (e.g., International SOS, WorldAware/OnSolve) offer 24/7 assistance centers, security advice, and emergency response for traveling employees, overlapping with Bond’s enterprise safety value proposition.

### Physical security and executive protection

- Traditional executive protection and security guard firms that provide bodyguards and physical details are indirect competitors; Bond emphasizes remote, AI‑enhanced monitoring plus the ability to arrange on‑the‑ground protection as needed. [nasdaq](https://www.nasdaq.com/press-release/bond-highlights-ai-powered-preventative-personal-security-scalable-alternative)

Because Bond’s positioning (“AI‑powered preventative personal security as a service”) is somewhat unique, they tend to compete against a *bundle* of point solutions (safety apps, hotlines, travel security, executive protection) rather than a single direct rival. [nasdaq](https://www.nasdaq.com/press-release/bond-highlights-ai-powered-preventative-personal-security-scalable-alternative)
