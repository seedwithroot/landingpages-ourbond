# Video Ads / Hooks

**Purpose:** First 5 seconds of video. The attention gate. Decides who enters the funnel.

---

## Hook Types to Test

### Problem-Led Hooks
Open with the pain point. Viewer recognizes themselves immediately.

**Hook 1: Walking Home Alone**
- **First frame:** Lone woman on dark street, looking at phone
- **Text overlay:** "Walking home alone?"
- **Voiceover:** "Walking home alone shouldn't feel this scary."
- **Next:** "Bond puts a live security agent with you..."

**Hook 2: Kids Not Responding**
- **First frame:** Parent looking at phone, concerned expression
- **Text overlay:** "She's not responding."
- **Voiceover:** "She's not where she should be. She's not responding. How do you help?"
- **Next:** "Bond shows real-time location and sends agents if needed..."

**Hook 3: Uncomfortable Situation**
- **First frame:** Person in crowded area, subtle discomfort
- **Text overlay:** "That feeling when something's off"
- **Voiceover:** "When uncomfortable becomes dangerous, it's too late for 911."
- **Next:** "Bond acts before situations escalate..."

---

### Outcome-Led Hooks
Lead with the result. Create aspiration.

**Hook 4: Never Walk Alone**
- **First frame:** Confident woman walking, phone glowing
- **Text overlay:** "Never walk alone again."
- **Voiceover:** "What if you never had to walk alone again?"
- **Next:** "Bond's live agents watch your route until you're safely home..."

**Hook 5: Someone's Always There**
- **First frame:** Bond app interface, "Agent Connected" indicator
- **Text overlay:** "Someone's always watching over you"
- **Voiceover:** "24/7. Someone's always there."
- **Next:** "Bond puts real security agents in your pocket..."

---

### Question Hooks
Sharp, direct question. Feels personal.

**Hook 6: What If You Could?**
- **First frame:** Person walking confidently at night
- **Text overlay:** "What if you had a bodyguard in your pocket?"
- **Voiceover:** "What if you had a bodyguard you could afford?"
- **Next:** "Bond gives you 24/7 security agents for less than $1/day..."

**Hook 7: Who's Watching Your Kids?**
- **First frame:** Map showing kids' locations
- **Text overlay:** "Do you know where your kids are right now?"
- **Voiceover:** "Do you know where your kids are right now? Not just their location - are they safe?"
- **Next:** "Bond tracks them and sends agents if something's wrong..."

---

### Surprising Visual Hooks
Pattern interrupt. Stop the scroll.

**Hook 8: One Button**
- **First frame:** Extreme close-up of finger hovering over "Ready An Agent" button
- **Text overlay:** (none - pure visual)
- **Voiceover:** (silence for 2 seconds, then) "One button. Real agent. Seconds."
- **Next:** "Press and hold. A security expert responds..."

**Hook 9: Split Screen Agent**
- **First frame:** Split screen - user walking + agent watching on monitor
- **Text overlay:** "She's not alone."
- **Voiceover:** "She thinks she's walking alone. She's not."
- **Next:** "Bond agents monitor every step..."

---

### Brand/Authority First Hooks
Best for retargeting. Lead with credibility.

**Hook 10: Former NYPD Commissioner**
- **First frame:** Raymond Kelly headshot or serious professional
- **Text overlay:** "Former NYPD Commissioner Raymond Kelly recommends Bond"
- **Voiceover:** Kelly audio (if available) or narrator
- **Next:** "Why experts trust Bond for personal security..."

**Hook 11: Secret Service Advised**
- **First frame:** Professional security aesthetic, badge visible
- **Text overlay:** "Advised by the former Director of the U.S. Secret Service"
- **Voiceover:** "Elite security. Made accessible."
- **Next:** "Bond brings professional protection to everyone..."

---

## Testing Priority

**Build first (cold audiences):**
1. Hook 1: Walking Home Alone (problem-led, individual)
2. Hook 4: Never Walk Alone (outcome-led, individual)
3. Hook 6: Bodyguard in Your Pocket (question, affordability angle)

**Build second (family audiences):**
4. Hook 2: Kids Not Responding (problem-led, family)
5. Hook 7: Who's Watching Your Kids (question, family)

**Build third (retargeting):**
6. Hook 10: NYPD Commissioner (authority, warm audiences)

---

## 5-Second Test Checklist

For each hook, verify:
- [ ] Viewer understands the topic within 5 seconds
- [ ] Clear who it's for (individual, family, specific situation)
- [ ] Creates curiosity, emotion, or recognition
- [ ] First frame carries the message (works with sound off)
- [ ] No wasted time (logo animations, slow setup, vague intro)
